# Module 3: Banking & Savings Strategies

## Title: Account Optimization and High-Yield Savings

### Lecture Content

Choosing the right banking products and maximizing your savings rate are crucial for building wealth. This module covers account types, savings optimization, and automation strategies.

#### 1. Types of Bank Accounts

**Checking Account:**
- Daily transactions
- Debit card access
- Usually low or no interest
- Easy access to funds

**Savings Account:**
- Interest-bearing
- Limited transactions (usually 6/month)
- Higher interest than checking
- Emergency fund location

**Money Market Account:**
- Higher interest than savings
- Check-writing privileges
- Higher minimum balance requirements
- FDIC insured

**High-Yield Savings Account:**
- Online banks offer best rates
- 10-20x traditional bank rates
- Easy access
- No minimum balance (often)

#### 2. Choosing the Right Bank

**Traditional Banks:**
- Pros: Physical branches, full-service
- Cons: Lower interest rates, fees

**Online Banks:**
- Pros: Higher interest rates, lower fees
- Cons: No physical branches, limited services

**Credit Unions:**
- Pros: Member-owned, lower fees
- Cons: Membership requirements, limited locations

**Considerations:**
- Interest rates
- Fees (monthly, ATM, overdraft)
- Minimum balance requirements
- ATM network access
- Online/mobile banking features

#### 3. Savings Account Optimization

**High-Yield Savings Strategies:**
- Compare rates regularly
- Look for promotional rates
- Consider online banks
- Avoid accounts with fees
- Check FDIC insurance

**Multiple Account Strategy:**
- Emergency fund (high-yield savings)
- Short-term goals (separate savings)
- Long-term goals (investment accounts)

**Interest Rate Comparison:**
- Traditional bank: 0.01-0.05% APY
- Online bank: 3-5% APY
- Difference: €100,000 × 4% = €4,000/year vs €50/year

#### 4. Automation Strategies

**Pay Yourself First:**
- Set up automatic transfers
- Transfer on payday
- Start with small amounts
- Increase over time

**Automation Benefits:**
- Removes decision fatigue
- Ensures consistency
- Builds habits
- Reduces temptation to spend

**What to Automate:**
- Emergency fund contributions
- Retirement contributions
- Bill payments
- Savings goal contributions

#### 5. Account Fees to Avoid

**Common Fees:**
- Monthly maintenance fees
- ATM fees (use in-network)
- Overdraft fees (opt-out or monitor)
- Minimum balance fees
- Wire transfer fees

**How to Avoid:**
- Meet minimum balance requirements
- Use in-network ATMs
- Set up low balance alerts
- Choose fee-free accounts
- Monitor account regularly

#### 6. Building Savings Habits

**Start Small:**
- €25-50 per month
- Increase gradually
- Focus on consistency

**Visualize Progress:**
- Track savings goals
- Celebrate milestones
- Review regularly

**Make it Automatic:**
- Set up recurring transfers
- Increase with raises
- Don't touch savings
