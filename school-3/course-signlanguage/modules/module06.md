# Module 6: Emotions & Descriptions

## Title: Expressing Feelings and Describing in ASL

### Lecture Content

Emotions and descriptions bring life to your signing! ASL is particularly expressive, using facial expressions, body language, and signs to convey feelings and describe people, places, and things.

#### 1. Basic Emotions
**HAPPY:**
- Both hands, open, brush up chest (like happiness rising)
- Facial expression: Big smile

**SAD:**
- Both hands, open, move down face (like tears)
- Facial expression: Frown, downturned mouth

**ANGRY/MAD:**
- Claw handshape, moves up from stomach
- Facial expression: Furrowed brows, intense look

**EXCITED:**
- Both hands, open, shake (like excitement)
- Facial expression: Wide eyes, big smile

**SCARED/AFRAID:**
- Both hands, open, move to chest (like protecting)
- Facial expression: Wide eyes, open mouth

**SURPRISED:**
- Both hands, open, move up (like shock)
- Facial expression: Wide eyes, raised eyebrows

**TIRED:**
- Both hands, Y handshapes, move down (like exhaustion)
- Facial expression: Droopy eyes

**BORED:**
- Index finger, circles near nose (like yawning)
- Facial expression: Blank, uninterested

**WORRIED:**
- Both hands, open, move in small circles near chest
- Facial expression: Concerned look

**CALM/PEACEFUL:**
- Both hands, open, move down slowly
- Facial expression: Relaxed, serene

#### 2. More Complex Emotions
**LOVE:**
- Both hands, crossed on chest (hug yourself)
- Facial expression: Warm smile

**LIKE:**
- Flat hand, palm on chest, moves forward
- Facial expression: Pleasant

**DISLIKE:**
- NOT + LIKE
- Or: Thumb down

**HATE:**
- Both hands, claw handshapes, scratch forward
- Facial expression: Intense, negative

**JEALOUS:**
- Index finger, moves from eye outward (like green-eyed)
- Facial expression: Slight frown

**PROUD:**
- Both hands, open, move up chest
- Facial expression: Confident smile

**EMBARRASSED:**
- Both hands, move up face (like hiding)
- Facial expression: Blushing, looking away

**CONFUSED:**
- Both hands, index fingers, circle near head
- Facial expression: Puzzled look

**FRUSTRATED:**
- Both hands, S handshapes, move forward forcefully
- Facial expression: Tense, annoyed

#### 3. Colors
**RED:**
- Index finger, moves down chin (like red lipstick)

**BLUE:**
- B handshape, shakes side to side

**GREEN:**
- G handshape, shakes side to side

**YELLOW:**
- Y handshape, shakes side to side

**ORANGE:**
- C handshape, near mouth, squeezes (like orange)

**PURPLE:**
- P handshape, shakes side to side

**PINK:**
- P handshape, moves down chin (like lipstick)

**BLACK:**
- Index finger, moves across forehead

**WHITE:**
- Open hand, fingers together, moves forward from chest

**BROWN:**
- B handshape, moves down cheek (like brown skin)

**GRAY/GREY:**
- Both hands, open, move back and forth (like mixing)

#### 4. Size and Shape
**BIG/LARGE:**
- Both hands, open, move apart (showing size)

**SMALL/LITTLE:**
- Both hands, open, come together (showing small size)

**TALL:**
- Flat hand, palm down, moves up (showing height)

**SHORT:**
- Flat hand, palm down, moves down (showing short)

**WIDE:**
- Both hands, open, move apart horizontally

**NARROW/THIN:**
- Both hands, index fingers, close together

**LONG:**
- Both hands, index fingers, move apart

**ROUND:**
- Both hands, form circle shape

**SQUARE:**
- Both hands, index fingers, form square

**THICK:**
- Both hands, C handshapes, show thickness

**THIN:**
- Both hands, index fingers, close together

#### 5. Describing People
**TALL:**
- Flat hand, palm down, moves up

**SHORT:**
- Flat hand, palm down, moves down

**FAT:**
- Both hands, C handshapes, show large size

**THIN/SKINNY:**
- Both hands, index fingers, close together

**YOUNG:**
- Both hands, open, move up (like youth)

**OLD:**
- C handshape, moves down (like aging)

**BEAUTIFUL/PRETTY:**
- Open hand, fingers together, moves down face (like beauty)

**HANDSOME:**
- Open hand, moves down face, then forward

**UGLY:**
- X handshape, moves down face (opposite of beautiful)

**NICE/KIND:**
- Flat hand, palm down, brushes down chest

**MEAN:**
- Index finger, points forward aggressively

**SMART/INTELLIGENT:**
- Index finger, touches forehead, moves forward

**FUNNY:**
- Index finger, touches nose, moves forward (like laughing)

#### 6. Describing Things
**GOOD:**
- Flat hand, palm down, moves away from mouth

**BAD:**
- Flat hand, palm down, moves down (opposite of good)

**NEW:**
- Both hands, open, move forward (like new)

**OLD:**
- C handshape, moves down

**CLEAN:**
- Both hands, open, wipe across (like cleaning)

**DIRTY:**
- Both hands, open, move down (like dirt)

**HOT:**
- C handshape, moves away from mouth (like hot breath)

**COLD:**
- Both hands, S handshapes, shake (like shivering)

**FAST:**
- Index finger, moves forward quickly

**SLOW:**
- Both hands, open, move forward slowly

**EASY:**
- Both hands, open, move forward easily

**HARD/DIFFICULT:**
- Both hands, S handshapes, tap together (like hard)

**IMPORTANT:**
- Both hands, index fingers, move up (like importance)

**INTERESTING:**
- Index finger, circles near head (like thinking)

**BORING:**
- Index finger, circles near nose (like yawning)

#### 7. Intensity and Degree
**VERY:**
- V handshape, moves forward (emphasize)

**REALLY:**
- R handshape, moves forward

**SO:**
- S handshape, moves forward

**A LITTLE/BIT:**
- Index finger and thumb, small space

**TOO MUCH:**
- Both hands, open, move apart (like too much)

**ENOUGH:**
- Both hands, open, tap together (like enough)

**MORE:**
- Both hands, fingertips together, move apart

**LESS:**
- Both hands, open, come together

**MOST:**
- Both hands, open, move up (like highest)

**LEAST:**
- Both hands, open, move down (like lowest)

#### 8. Expressive Signing Techniques
**Size and Shape Modifiers:**
- Use space to show size
- Exaggerate for emphasis
- Use both hands to show relationships

**Facial Expressions:**
- Match emotion to sign
- Intensity shows degree
- Use whole face, not just mouth

**Body Language:**
- Lean in for intensity
- Step back for distance
- Use body to show perspective

**Repetition:**
- Repeat signs for emphasis
- Vary speed for intensity
- Use pauses for effect

**Example:**
- "VERY HAPPY" - Sign HAPPY with big smile, repeat, use more space
- "A LITTLE SAD" - Sign SAD softly, small movement, slight frown

#### 9. Comparing and Contrasting
**SAME:**
- Both hands, index fingers, tap together

**DIFFERENT:**
- Both hands, index fingers, cross and separate

**SIMILAR:**
- Both hands, open, move together (like similar)

**BETTER:**
- Flat hand, palm down, moves up (like improvement)

**WORSE:**
- Flat hand, palm down, moves down (like decline)

**BEST:**
- Flat hand, palm down, moves up high

**WORST:**
- Flat hand, palm down, moves down low

**MORE THAN:**
- MORE + THAN
- THAN: Index finger, moves forward

**LESS THAN:**
- LESS + THAN

#### 10. Putting It All Together
**Example Sentences:**
- "I VERY HAPPY TODAY" - Sign with big smile, emphasize VERY
- "SHE TALL, BEAUTIFUL, SMART" - Describe with appropriate expressions
- "PIZZA HOT, DELICIOUS" - Show heat and enjoyment
- "MOVIE BORING, LONG" - Show boredom and length
- "MY CAR SMALL, RED, OLD" - Multiple descriptors

**Structure:** When describing, you can list multiple adjectives:
- "PERSON TALL, SMART, NICE"
- "FOOD HOT, SPICY, DELICIOUS"

### Cultural Note
In Deaf culture:
- Facial expressions are grammatical, not just emotional
- Direct expression of emotions is common and valued
- Body language and space are important
- Visual expression is highly developed
- Authenticity in expression is appreciated
