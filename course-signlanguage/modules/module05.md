# Module 5: Time & Calendar

## Title: Expressing Time in ASL

### Lecture Content

Time concepts are essential for scheduling, telling stories, and daily communication. ASL has specific ways to express time that differ from English.

#### 1. Days of the Week
**MONDAY:**
- M handshape, circles forward

**TUESDAY:**
- T handshape, circles forward

**WEDNESDAY:**
- W handshape, circles forward

**THURSDAY:**
- H handshape, circles forward

**FRIDAY:**
- F handshape, circles forward

**SATURDAY:**
- S handshape, circles forward

**SUNDAY:**
- Both hands, open, palms facing each other, move in circle

**DAY:**
- Non-dominant arm, palm down; dominant index finger, moves along arm (like sun moving)

**WEEK:**
- One hand, palm down; other hand's index finger, moves across (like week passing)

#### 2. Months
**JANUARY:**
- J handshape, circles forward

**FEBRUARY:**
- F handshape, circles forward

**MARCH:**
- M handshape, circles forward

**APRIL:**
- A handshape, circles forward

**MAY:**
- M handshape, circles forward

**JUNE:**
- J handshape, circles forward

**JULY:**
- J handshape, circles forward

**AUGUST:**
- A handshape, circles forward

**SEPTEMBER:**
- S handshape, circles forward

**OCTOBER:**
- O handshape, circles forward

**NOVEMBER:**
- N handshape, circles forward

**DECEMBER:**
- D handshape, circles forward

**MONTH:**
- Index finger, moves down (like months passing)

**YEAR:**
- Both hands, S handshapes, circle around each other

#### 3. Seasons
**SPRING:**
- Both hands, open, move up (like growth)

**SUMMER:**
- Index finger, wipes across forehead (like sweat)

**FALL/AUTUMN:**
- Both hands, move down (like leaves falling)

**WINTER:**
- Both hands, shake (like shivering)

#### 4. Time Expressions
**NOW:**
- Both Y handshapes, move down together

**TODAY:**
- Both hands, open, palms up, move down (like "this day")

**YESTERDAY:**
- Y handshape, moves back over shoulder

**TOMORROW:**
- A handshape, moves forward from chin

**DAY BEFORE YESTERDAY:**
- YESTERDAY + BEFORE

**DAY AFTER TOMORROW:**
- TOMORROW + AFTER

**THIS WEEK:**
- NOW + WEEK

**LAST WEEK:**
- WEEK + moves back

**NEXT WEEK:**
- WEEK + moves forward

**THIS MONTH:**
- NOW + MONTH

**LAST MONTH:**
- MONTH + moves back

**NEXT MONTH:**
- MONTH + moves forward

**THIS YEAR:**
- NOW + YEAR

**LAST YEAR:**
- YEAR + moves back

**NEXT YEAR:**
- YEAR + moves forward

#### 5. Time of Day
**MORNING:**
- Flat hand, palm up, moves across body (like sun rising)

**AFTERNOON:**
- Flat hand, palm down, moves down from forehead

**EVENING:**
- Flat hand, palm down, moves down

**NIGHT:**
- Flat hand, palm down, moves down

**NOON:**
- Flat hand, palm down, moves across (like sun at peak)

**MIDNIGHT:**
- NIGHT + MIDDLE

**DAWN:**
- MORNING + EARLY

**DUSK:**
- EVENING + LATE

#### 6. Clock Time
**TIME:**
- Index finger, taps wrist (like watch)

**HOUR:**
- Index finger, points to wrist, then moves forward

**MINUTE:**
- Index finger, moves forward (smaller movement than hour)

**O'CLOCK:**
- Index finger, circles (like clock face)

**Examples:**
- 3 O'CLOCK: Show 3, then O'CLOCK
- 9:30: Show 9, then 30, or 9 + HALF (half handshape)
- 2:15: Show 2, then 15, or 2 + QUARTER

**Numbers for time:**
- Use regular number signs
- Can fingerspell for clarity: "3:45 PM"

#### 7. Temporal Markers
**BEFORE:**
- Flat hand, palm facing back, moves back

**AFTER:**
- Flat hand, palm facing forward, moves forward

**DURING:**
- Both hands, index fingers, move together (like overlapping)

**UNTIL:**
- Index finger, points forward, holds

**SINCE:**
- Index finger, points back, moves forward

**ALREADY:**
- Y handshape, moves down (like completed)

**NOT YET:**
- NOT + YET
- YET: Y handshape, moves up

**STILL:**
- Y handshape, holds in place

**FINISH:**
- Both hands, open, flip forward (like done)

**START/BEGIN:**
- Index finger, touches non-dominant palm, then moves forward

#### 8. Frequency
**EVERY DAY:**
- Flat hand, palm down, moves forward repeatedly

**EVERY WEEK:**
- EVERY + WEEK

**EVERY MONTH:**
- EVERY + MONTH

**EVERY YEAR:**
- EVERY + YEAR

**ONCE:**
- Index finger, points up, moves forward once

**TWICE:**
- Two fingers, move forward twice

**OFTEN:**
- Open hand, moves forward repeatedly

**SOMETIMES:**
- Index finger, moves up and down

**RARELY:**
- R handshape, moves forward slowly

**NEVER:**
- Open hand, moves across body (like cutting)

#### 9. ASL Time Structure
In ASL, time usually comes FIRST in sentences:

**English:** "I go to school every Monday"
**ASL:** "EVERY MONDAY, SCHOOL I GO"

**English:** "Yesterday I ate pizza"
**ASL:** "YESTERDAY, PIZZA I EAT"

**English:** "Tomorrow I will study"
**ASL:** "TOMORROW, STUDY I WILL" or "TOMORROW STUDY"

**Pattern:** TIME - TOPIC - COMMENT

#### 10. Scheduling and Appointments
**APPOINTMENT:**
- Both hands, A handshapes, tap together

**MEETING:**
- Both hands, come together (like meeting)

**SCHEDULE:**
- Both hands, open, move down (like calendar)

**BUSY:**
- Both hands, B handshapes, move back and forth

**FREE:**
- Both hands, F handshapes, move apart (like open)

**AVAILABLE:**
- Both hands, open, move forward

**Example:**
- "MONDAY 3:00, MEETING I HAVE"
- "TOMORROW AFTERNOON, FREE I"

### Cultural Note
In Deaf culture:
- Punctuality is often highly valued
- Visual schedules and calendars are common
- Time may be discussed more explicitly
- Events often have clear start/end times
- Being on time shows respect
