# Module 4: Questions & Conversations

## Title: Asking Questions and Having Conversations in ASL

### Lecture Content

Questions are essential for conversations! In ASL, questions use specific facial expressions and structures. This module teaches you how to ask and answer questions naturally.

#### 1. Yes/No Questions
Yes/no questions require:
- **Eyebrows raised** (up)
- **Head tilted forward** slightly
- **Eyes wide open**
- **Hold the last sign** (don't drop hands immediately)

**Common Yes/No Question Signs:**

**YOU:**
- Point to person

**HE/SHE:**
- Point to location where person is established

**THEY:**
- Point to multiple locations

**HAVE:**
- Both hands, palms up, move toward body

**CAN:**
- Both hands, S handshapes, move down together

**WILL:**
- Both hands, open, move forward

**DO:**
- Both hands, index fingers, move forward

**ARE/IS:**
- Index finger, points up

**Example Questions:**
- YOU HUNGRY? (eyebrows up)
- YOU HAVE CAR? (eyebrows up)
- YOU CAN SWIM? (eyebrows up)
- YOU LIKE PIZZA? (eyebrows up)

#### 2. WH-Questions (Who, What, Where, When, Why, How)
WH-questions require:
- **Eyebrows furrowed** (down)
- **Head tilted forward**
- **Slight squint** or intense look
- **Hold the question sign**

**WHO:**
- Index finger, circles near mouth (like calling someone)

**WHAT:**
- Open hand, palm up, shakes side to side
- Or: Index finger, shakes side to side

**WHERE:**
- Index finger, shakes side to side (different from WHAT - check context)

**WHEN:**
- Index finger, circles near non-dominant hand (like clock)

**WHY:**
- Y handshape, touches forehead, moves forward (like thinking)

**HOW:**
- Both hands, palms up, move up and down alternately

**HOW MUCH/HOW MANY:**
- HOW + MUCH/MANY
- MUCH: Both hands, C handshapes, move apart
- MANY: Both hands, open, fingers wiggle

**Example Questions:**
- WHAT YOUR NAME? (eyebrows down)
- WHERE YOU FROM? (eyebrows down)
- WHEN YOU GO? (eyebrows down)
- WHY YOU SAD? (eyebrows down)
- HOW YOU? (eyebrows down)
- WHO THAT? (eyebrows down)

#### 3. Answering Questions
**YES:**
- S handshape, nods up and down (like head nodding)
- Or: Open hand, palm forward, moves forward

**NO:**
- Index and middle fingers, tap together, then separate
- Or: Open hand, palm forward, moves side to side

**MAYBE:**
- Both hands, open, palms up, move up and down alternately

**DON'T KNOW:**
- KNOW sign, then shake head NO

**NOT:**
- Index and middle fingers, tap together under chin, then move forward

**Example Answers:**
- YOU HUNGRY? → YES, I HUNGRY
- YOU HAVE CAR? → NO, I NOT HAVE CAR
- WHERE YOU FROM? → I FROM [location]
- WHAT YOUR NAME? → MY NAME [name]

#### 4. Question Words in Context
**WHAT TIME:**
- WHAT + TIME
- TIME: Index finger, taps wrist (like watch)

**WHAT COLOR:**
- WHAT + COLOR
- COLOR: Fingers wiggle near chin

**WHAT DO:**
- WHAT + DO
- DO: Both hands, index fingers, move forward

**HOW LONG:**
- HOW + LONG
- LONG: Both hands, index fingers, move apart

**HOW OLD:**
- HOW + OLD
- OLD: Hand moves down (like aging)

#### 5. Conversation Strategies
**Clarification:**
- SORRY, NOT UNDERSTAND
- AGAIN PLEASE (sign again, then PLEASE)
- SLOW PLEASE
- SPELL PLEASE (fingerspell it)

**Asking for Repetition:**
- AGAIN: Both hands, open, move in circle (like repeating)
- WHAT: Open hand, palm up, shakes
- MEAN: Both hands, index fingers, tap together

**Confirming Understanding:**
- UNDERSTAND: Index finger touches forehead, moves forward
- RIGHT: Index finger, moves up (like checking)
- SAME: Both hands, index fingers, tap together

**Changing Topic:**
- OH I SEE: Index finger touches eye, moves forward
- DIFFERENT: Both hands, index fingers, cross and separate
- NEXT: Index finger, moves forward

#### 6. Conversation Flow
**Starting a conversation:**
1. Get attention
2. Greet
3. Ask a question or make a comment

**Maintaining conversation:**
- Ask follow-up questions
- Show interest (nod, facial expressions)
- Share related information
- Use back-channeling (nodding, facial expressions)

**Ending conversation:**
- Summarize or acknowledge
- Say goodbye
- Look away to signal end

**Example Conversation:**
- A: HELLO, HOW YOU?
- B: I FINE, THANK YOU. YOU?
- A: I GOOD. WHAT YOU DO?
- B: I STUDENT. YOU?
- A: I WORK. WHERE YOU STUDY?
- B: I STUDY [location]. YOU LIKE?
- A: YES, I LIKE. NICE TALK YOU.
- B: SAME, GOODBYE.
- A: GOODBYE.

#### 7. Non-Manual Markers for Questions
**Yes/No Questions:**
- Eyebrows: UP
- Head: Tilted forward
- Eyes: Wide open
- Mouth: Slightly open or neutral
- Hold: Last sign

**WH-Questions:**
- Eyebrows: DOWN (furrowed)
- Head: Tilted forward
- Eyes: Slightly squinted, intense
- Mouth: Can show intensity
- Hold: Question word sign

**Practice:** Same sentence, different facial expressions:
- YOU GO STORE? (yes/no - eyebrows up)
- WHERE YOU GO? (WH-question - eyebrows down)

#### 8. Rhetorical Questions
ASL uses rhetorical questions (questions you answer yourself):
- WHY? (then answer)
- Used for emphasis and flow
- Eyebrows down, but you answer immediately

**Example:**
- "YESTERDAY I GO STORE. WHY? BECAUSE I NEED FOOD."
- "I LIKE PIZZA. WHY? TASTE GOOD."

### Cultural Note
In Deaf culture:
- Direct questions are common and acceptable
- Eye contact during questions shows engagement
- Facial expressions are crucial - they're grammatical
- Asking for clarification is encouraged, not rude
- Conversations may feel more direct than hearing culture
